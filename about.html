Our Story: From Urgent Need to Lasting Solution

We are a dedicated team of Ugandan social workers, entrepreneurs, and community leaders who believe in the potential of our youth and the resilience of our mothers.

Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To equip young people (ages 12-30) with the values, health knowledge, and economic tools necessary to build stable futures, create their own opportunities, and break the cycle of single motherhood.

Our Vision

To build a generation where both boys and girls grow into responsible adults, informed decision-makers, and engaged parents, fostering strong families and thriving communities.

The Problem in Detail

The challenge is immense. 30% of Ugandan women (18-35) are now single mothers, a sharp increase from just a decade ago. This crisis is fueled by a complex mix of factors:

  • High Teen Pregnancy: In regions like Bukedi (29.5%) and Gulu (50% of hospital deliveries), girls are becoming mothers before they finish school.
  • Economic Dependency: A lack of skills and jobs, with over 400,000 youth entering the job market for only 9,000 formal jobs, leaves women vulnerable.
  • Absent Fatherhood: A cultural and economic crisis of absent or disengaged fathers leaves mothers to cope alone.
  • Weak Support: A lack of affordable childcare and legal support traps women in poverty.
